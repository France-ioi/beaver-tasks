Gattaca

Each of the 30 face down cards below contains an "a", "c", "g" or "t". They represent part of a Beaver's DNA.

Somewhere in the DNA of the beaver the sequence gattaca is hidden. You have to try to find it by clicking on the cards to reveal which letter is on the other side.

While you click the computer will try to work against you so you can't get too lucky.

Try it! Click on the cards to flip them over. Try to find the sequence in as few clicks as possible.

You can try to improve your score as many times as you want.

Solution

There are several approaches to solve this problem, but the main idea is generally to find ways to discard subsequences of cards and finding that they can't contain the word "gattaca", while uncovering only some of the letters in these subsequences.

For example, if we click on the letter 7 and find a letter "c", then you can already conclude that the first letter can't be part of the word "gattaca". If you then click on the letter 8 and find a letter "t", then you can conclude that none of the letters until that "t" can be part of the solution, as the sequence "ct" does not appear in "gattaca". You can then continue with this strategy, and click on the 15h letter.

Since getting the correct answer to some degree depends on luck, you can get full points if you have used 12 steps whereas the optimal answer would only need 10 steps.

This strategy can be synthetised as the following : "At each step, click on the last card that may invalidate the first card that is not yet invalidated".

C'est de l'informatique !

This tasks consists in finding and applying an efficient algorithm to search for an occurrence of a word in a set of strings.

The algorithm described in the solution resembles a well known string search algorithm: Boyer-Moore.
